## 1. Core Doctrine

This document defines the foundational architecture for HeelKawn's simulation engine, establishing a clear hierarchy from objective reality to emergent action.

-   **Reality exists before interpretation**: The world's physical state, events, and existence are independent of any observer's perception.
-   **WorldMemory records objective facts**: The `WorldMemory` system serves as the immutable, unbiased ledger of all observable occurrences. It states "what happened," not "what it means."
-   **WorldMeaning derives neutral descriptive meaning tokens**: The `WorldMeaning` system acts as a neutral "Meaning Engine." It processes raw facts from `WorldMemory` and extracts objective, descriptive "Meaning Tokens" without assigning moral, ethical, or philosophical judgment. These tokens describe the nature of events and their immediate, quantifiable implications.
-   **ReligionSystem, AshaDrujSystem, CultureSystem, PoliticsSystem, MythSystem, books, NPC minds, and future institutions interpret those tokens differently**: All higher-order concepts, beliefs, and values emerge from various interpreters consuming the same stream of neutral Meaning Tokens. Each interpreter applies its unique framework (personality, culture, religion, etc.) to form beliefs and pressures.
-   **Asha/Druj is one interpretation layer, not the foundation of the engine**: The Asha/Druj framework is a specific philosophical lens through which Meaning Tokens can be interpreted. It is not intrinsic to the engine's core functioning.
-   **No morality meter**: The engine itself does not possess an inherent moral compass. Moral judgments are emergent properties of specific interpretation systems.
-   **No chosen-one logic**: All events and their interpretations arise from the interactions within the simulation. No entity is pre-ordained or intrinsically privileged by the core systems.
-   **No authored prophecy outcomes**: Prophecies must emerge dynamically from patterns and interpretations of Meaning Tokens, rather than being pre-scripted.
-   **Myths must emerge from history, not overwrite history**: Belief systems, narratives, and myths are generated by interpreting `WorldMemory` facts, ensuring they are grounded in the simulation's history.

## 2. Layer Definitions

### **Reality**

-   **What it owns**: All objective, quantifiable, and immutable aspects of the simulation world. This includes terrain, weather, time, resources, flora, fauna, objects, buildings, physics, and fundamental biological/physical processes (birth, death, growth, decay).
-   **What it must never own**: Any form of interpretation, opinion, or subjective meaning. It does not determine "good" or "bad."
-   **Current repo system**: No single system "owns" reality; it is the aggregate state managed by various core simulation systems (e.g., `World`, `TerrainSystem`, `PawnSystem` for physical existence).
-   **Future system**: N/A (fundamental layer).

### **Facts**

-   **What it owns**: Discrete, timestamped, objective records of events occurring within Reality. These records capture "what happened" with relevant objective parameters (who, what, where, when, quantities).
-   **What it must never own**: Interpretation, judgment, emotional valence, or philosophical implications.
-   **Current repo system**: `autoloads/WorldMemory.gd`.
-   **Future system**: N/A (fundamental layer).

### **WorldMemory**

-   **What it owns**: The immutable, append-only ledger of all `Facts` in the simulation. Provides an API for recording and retrieving facts.
-   **What it must never own**: Any logic for interpreting facts, deriving meaning, or influencing agent behavior beyond simply providing factual data.
-   **Current repo system**: `autoloads/WorldMemory.gd`.
-   **Future system**: N/A (fundamental layer).

### **Memory Holders**

-   **What it owns**: The selective retention, storage, and retrieval of facts (or summaries of facts) by specific entities or mechanisms. This involves filtering and contextualization of `WorldMemory` facts.
-   **What it must never own**: The original, objective `WorldMemory` facts. It may hold *interpretations* of facts, but not modify the facts themselves.
-   **Current repo system**:
    -   `PawnMind` (FUTURE): For individual pawns, their direct experiences and remembered facts.
    -   `WorldMeaning` (as token aggregations): For collective, summarized memory (e.g., settlement history).
    -   `ChronicleFeed` (FUTURE): For long-term historical records and narratives.
-   **Future system**: `PawnMind` (expanded), `ChronicleSystem` (consolidated).

### **WorldMeaning**

-   **What it owns**: The sole responsibility for processing raw `WorldMemory` facts and deriving neutral, descriptive "Meaning Tokens." It aggregates these tokens across various scopes (pawn, family, settlement, region, global) and provides queryable access to these aggregations.
-   **What it must never own**: Any logic that interprets Meaning Tokens through a specific philosophical, ethical, or moral lens. It must not form beliefs or directly influence agent actions based on these tokens.
-   **Current repo system**: `autoloads/WorldMeaning.gd` (needs significant refactoring).
-   **Future system**: N/A (fundamental layer).

### **Meaning Tokens**

-   **What it owns**: Objective, descriptive keywords or tags extracted from `WorldMemory` facts, representing their inherent characteristics or implications without moral judgment.
-   **What it must never own**: Any subjective value, moral valence, or philosophical bias.
-   **Current repo system**: Generated by `WorldMeaning` (FUTURE).
-   **Future system**: N/A (data layer).

### **Interpretation**

-   **What it owns**: The process by which Meaning Tokens (and their aggregations) are consumed and filtered through a specific philosophical, ethical, cultural, or individual lens to form beliefs, desires, goals, and pressures.
-   **What it must never own**: The generation of Meaning Tokens or the modification of `WorldMemory` facts.
-   **Current repo system**:
    -   `AshaDrujSystem` (will interpret Meaning Tokens to derive Asha/Druj scores).
    -   `ReligionSystem` (will interpret Meaning Tokens to form religious beliefs, Karma, Dharma).
    -   `PawnMind` (FUTURE): For individual pawns, interprets tokens based on personality, education, etc.
-   **Future system**: `PoliticsSystem`, `CultureSystem`, `EconomicsSystem`, `MythSystem`, `HistorianSystem`, `BardSystem`, `ProphecySystem`, `BookSystem` (all consuming Meaning Tokens).

### **Action**

-   **What it owns**: The decision-making processes of pawns and simulation systems, driven by their current internal states, beliefs, desires, and pressures (all ultimately derived from interpretations of Meaning Tokens). Actions manifest as changes in Reality, which in turn generate new Facts.
-   **What it must never own**: The generation of Facts or Meaning Tokens.
-   **Current repo system**: Various `Pawn` behaviors, `PawnSystem` decisions, `Settlement` logic, `World` event triggers.
-   **Future system**: N/A (fundamental layer).

## 3. Meaning Token Rules

Meaning Tokens are neutral descriptors of reality, derived from `WorldMemory` facts. They are predicates or adjectives of events, not judgments.

**General Rules for Meaning Tokens:**

-   **Descriptive, Not Prescriptive**: Tokens describe *what is*, not *what should be*.
-   **Context-Sensitive**: The same `WorldMemory` fact can generate different tokens based on its context (e.g., resource levels, participants).
-   **Atomic & Composable**: Tokens should ideally represent a single concept, but can be combined to describe complex events.
-   **Quantifiable/Trendable**: Aggregations of tokens should allow for statistical analysis (counts, frequencies, trends) over time and scope.

**Example Token Categories and Aggregation Scopes:**

| Token Category | Example Tokens | WorldMemory Fact Generation | Aggregation Scope(s) | Why Descriptive, Not Moral |
| :------------- | :------------- | :-------------------------- | :------------------- | :------------------------- |

## 4. Interpreter Rules

Interpreters are systems or individual pawn minds that process neutral Meaning Tokens and apply a subjective framework to derive beliefs, values, and desired actions.

**Hard Rule**: Interpreters may create beliefs, desires, rituals, policies, myths, and actions. **Interpreters must not rewrite WorldMemory facts.**

**Common Mechanisms for Interpretation:**

-   **Token Mapping**: Each interpreter defines how specific Meaning Tokens (or combinations thereof) contribute to its internal metrics, beliefs, or emotional states.
-   **Contextual Weighting**: The impact of a Meaning Token can vary based on the interpreter's current internal state, historical context (from its own memory), or other aggregated tokens.
-   **Pattern Recognition**: Interpreters can identify recurring patterns or trends in Meaning Tokens, leading to emergent beliefs or policies.

**Example Interpreters:**

-   **`AshaDrujSystem`**:
    -   **Consumes**: `truth_spoken`, `cooperation`, `preservation_act`, `mercy_act`, `stewardship` (-> Asha pressure). `deception_act`, `betrayal`, `violence_act`, `destruction_act`, `corruption` (-> Druj pressure).
    -   **Output**: Global Asha/Druj scores, pawn/settlement Asha/Druj alignment values. Triggers `asha_excess`, `druj_excess` events based on these scores.
    -   **Perspective**: A cosmic balance, truth vs. chaos.
-   **`ReligionSystem`**:
    -   **Consumes**: `sacrifice`, `hope_trigger`, `abundance`, `natural_disaster`, `death`, `birth`, `healing`.
    -   **Output**: God favor/disfavor, Karma scores, Dharma indices, religious rituals, taboos, sacred history. Drives god emergence and pawn devotion.
    -   **Perspective**: Divine will, spiritual consequences, moral order.
-   **`PoliticsSystem` (FUTURE)**:
    -   **Consumes**: `authority_exercise`, `rebellion_act`, `scarcity`, `abundance`, `violence_act`, `trade_transaction`, `law_enforcement`.
    -   **Output**: Factional power, settlement stability, policy decisions, laws, war/peace declarations.
    -   **Perspective**: Power dynamics, social order, resource allocation.
-   **`CultureSystem` (FUTURE)**:
    -   **Consumes**: `kinship_interaction`, `trade_transaction`, `migration`, `innovation`, `knowledge_transfer`, `trauma_trigger`, `food_sharing`.
    -   **Output**: Cultural values, traditions, norms, taboos, artistic styles, language evolution.
    -   **Perspective**: Shared identity, social cohesion, inherited practices.
-   **`MythSystem` (FUTURE)**:
    -   **Consumes**: Long-term patterns and emotionally salient sequences of `Meaning Tokens` (e.g., repeated `natural_disaster` followed by `heroism` and `community_resilience`).
    -   **Output**: Emergent myths, legends, and archetypal narratives that explain historical events.
    -   **Perspective**: Narrative coherence, shared identity, moral lessons through story.
-   **Pawns (`PawnMind` - FUTURE)**:
    -   **Consumes**: All `Meaning Tokens` (especially those related to direct experience or local scope).
    -   **Output**: Individual beliefs, desires, goals, emotional states, opinions, ethical judgments.
    -   **Perspective**: Highly individualized, influenced by `personality`, `age`, `relationships`, `trauma`, `role`, `education`, `culture`, `religion`.

**Example: Opposite Conclusions from Identical Meaning Tokens:**

*   **Facts**: A drought leads to `scarcity` of water. A powerful pawn, King Theron, diverts the last river flow to his own fields, leading to `resource_gain` for him but increased `scarcity` and `famine_trigger` for commoners. This is followed by `rebellion_act` by commoners.
*   **Meaning Tokens (generated by `WorldMeaning`)**: `drought`, `scarcity`, `resource_diversion`, `resource_gain` (for Theron), `resource_loss` (for commoners), `famine_trigger`, `authority_exercise` (by Theron), `oppression_act`, `rebellion_act`, `violence_act` (during rebellion).

*   **Interpreter 1: Royal Historian (a `PawnMind` influenced by `PoliticsSystem` values)**:
    *   **Beliefs**: "The King is divinely appointed. Order is paramount."
    *   **Interpretation**: Focuses on `authority_exercise` (positive), `rebellion_act` (negative), `violence_act` (negative). Interprets `resource_diversion` as "wise resource management during crisis" necessary for leadership.
    *   **Conclusion**: "The King, in his wisdom, ensured stability, but rebellious elements sowed chaos. The drought was a test of faith."

*   **Interpreter 2: Peasant Revolutionary (a `PawnMind` influenced by `CultureSystem` values of equity)**:
    *   **Beliefs**: "All share in the land's bounty. Tyranny must be resisted."
    *   **Interpretation**: Focuses on `scarcity`, `famine_trigger`, `resource_loss` (negative), `oppression_act` (highly negative), `rebellion_act` (positive), `violence_act` (justified). Interprets `resource_diversion` as "selfish hoarding."
    *   **Conclusion**: "The King's greed brought famine. The people rose up against injustice. The drought was a catalyst for freedom."

## 5. Action Loop

This loop describes the continuous cycle of cause and effect, where every layer eventually influences the observable world.

1.  **Reality Changes**: The physical state of the simulation world evolves (e.g., resources deplete, pawns move, weather shifts).
2.  **`WorldMemory` Fact Recorded**: Significant changes in Reality generate objective facts that are recorded in `WorldMemory.gd` (e.g., `pawn_killed_deer`, `settlement_founded`).
3.  **`WorldMeaning` Token Generated**: `WorldMeaning.gd` processes new `WorldMemory` facts, extracting neutral, descriptive Meaning Tokens (e.g., `violence_act`, `structure_creation`, `abundance`).
4.  **Tokens Aggregate by Scope**: `WorldMeaning.gd` aggregates these Meaning Tokens over time and across various scopes (pawn, family, settlement, region, global), providing summaries and trends.
5.  **Interpreters Form Beliefs/Pressures**: Various interpreters (e.g., `AshaDrujSystem`, `ReligionSystem`, `PawnMind`, `PoliticsSystem`) consume the raw Meaning Tokens and their aggregations from `WorldMeaning.gd`. They filter these through their unique philosophical/personal frameworks to form beliefs, update internal states (e.g., Asha/Druj scores, Karma, emotional states, resource needs, political pressures), and generate goals/desires.
6.  **Pawns/Systems Choose Actions**: Based on their current internal states, beliefs, desires, and goals (derived from interpretation), pawns and simulation systems select and execute actions.
7.  **Actions Create New Reality Changes**: These chosen actions manifest as observable changes in the simulation's Reality (e.g., a pawn builds a house, a settlement declares war).
8.  **New Facts Are Recorded**: These new Reality changes generate new objective facts, which are recorded in `WorldMemory.gd`, completing the loop.

This cycle ensures that meaning is never just analytics; it continuously feeds back into the simulation to drive emergent behavior and shape future history.

## 6. Performance Rules

To ensure scalability and maintain determinism, the following performance rules must be adhered to during implementation:

-   **`WorldMeaning` processes only new `WorldMemory` events**: Avoid re-processing the entire `WorldMemory` history each tick. Use an internal cursor (`_last_processed_world_event_index`) to track progress.
-   **No full-history scan every tick**: Neither `WorldMeaning` nor any interpreter should perform full scans of `WorldMemory` every tick.
-   **Use cursor/index tracking**: `WorldMeaning` must efficiently track the last processed event index from `WorldMemory`.
-   **Use deterministic batching**: `WorldMeaning` should process `WorldMemory` events in deterministic batches (e.g., `MAX_EVENTS_PER_MEANING_PASS`) per tick to prevent CPU spikes.
-   **Use tick-throttled processing**: System-level interpreters (e.g., `ReligionSystem`, `PoliticsSystem`) should consume Meaning Token aggregations on slower, configurable cadences, not every tick.
-   **Aggregate tokens by scope**: `WorldMeaning` should maintain pre-aggregated summaries of Meaning Tokens for various scopes (pawn, settlement, region, global) to allow efficient queries by interpreters.
-   **Pawns query relevant local summaries, not all history**: `PawnMind` should query `WorldMeaning` for aggregated Meaning Tokens relevant to their current location, social circle, or immediate context, rather than processing raw tokens or full history.
-   **Systems run on slower cadences where appropriate**: Optimize system updates to run only when necessary, or on a reduced frequency.
-   **Save/load must not double-process historical facts**: Upon loading a saved game, `WorldMemory` and `WorldMeaning` must correctly restore their internal indices (`_last_processed_world_event_index`) to prevent re-processing already interpreted events.

## 7. Richard’s THIS OR THAT Alignment

This architecture enshrines Richard's core doctrines into the simulation's fundamental laws, ensuring they are emergent properties of the engine rather than hardcoded lore.

-   **Choice creates consequence**: Pawns make choices (driven by interpretation of Meaning Tokens) that generate facts, which in turn generate new Meaning Tokens, feeding back into further interpretation and action. The loop is explicit.
-   **Truth precedes interpretation**: `WorldMemory` holds the objective truth (facts). `WorldMeaning` transforms these into neutral descriptors (tokens). All subjective interpretation happens *after* this objective foundation.
-   **Purpose is deeper than occupation**: Purpose emerges from a pawn's unique interpretation of Meaning Tokens, influencing their goals and actions, transcending mere role-playing of their profession.
-   **Civilization remembers actions**: `WorldMemory` records all actions. `WorldMeaning` provides aggregated tokens that form the basis of collective memory, traditions, and institutions, allowing civilizations to "remember" and react to their history.
-   **Deception/corruption are derived from behavior**: Meaning Tokens like `deception_act`, `corruption_act` are descriptive of observed behaviors recorded as facts. Their ethical judgment (e.g., as Druj pressure) is applied by specific interpreters, not by the core engine.
-   **Stewardship and responsibility emerge through action**: Tokens like `preservation_act`, `destruction_act`, `resource_management` describe behaviors. The concepts of "stewardship" or "responsibility" are then formed by interpreters who value certain token patterns.
-   **Inherited knowledge can be preserved, distorted, or lost**: `knowledge_transfer` is a Meaning Token. How it is remembered (via Memory Holders), interpreted (by PawnMind/CultureSystem), and potentially altered (through distortion in Memory) is explicitly modeled.
