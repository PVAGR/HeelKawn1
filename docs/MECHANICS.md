# HeelKawn Mechanics

## Overview

HeelKawn’s mechanics are built to support a persistent, deterministic society simulation. Systems should reinforce memory, economy, conflict, and long-lived consequence rather than short-lived gamey rewards.

This document groups the core mechanics around Krond, magic, war, and classes as social structure.

## Krond Economy

Krond is the world’s economic backbone. It should behave like a persistent value system tied to labor, storage, scarcity, trade, and settlement stability.

Core expectations:

- Krond is earned through meaningful contribution, not abstract reward drops.
- Resource flow should reflect real material pressure in the world.
- Settlements should accumulate or lose strength based on what they can store, produce, and protect.
- The economy should support long arcs such as trade, provisioning, defense, and craft specialization.

At a systems level, Krond should stay connected to actual inventory, logistics, and settlement needs. It should not become a detached currency minigame.

## Magic

Magic in HeelKawn is not a flashy power fantasy layer. It is a disciplined knowledge domain expressed through ritual, preservation, and rare practices that still obey deterministic causality.

Design constraints:

- Magic must emerge from world rules, not override them.
- Magical effects should have prerequisites, costs, and historical traces.
- Discovery should feel like learning, not random loot.
- Ritual knowledge should be preservable, teachable, and losable like any other cultural artifact.

The project’s canon consistently treats subtle metaphysics as world pressure rather than divine intervention, so magic should remain compatible with that tone.

## War

War is a social and logistical consequence of memory, scarcity, authority, and repeated conflict. It should be treated as a systemic outcome, not a spectacle toggle.

War mechanics should account for:

- Local grievance and conflict density.
- Settlement posture and defensive infrastructure.
- Resource pressure and survival logic.
- Chain reactions from reputation, fear, and retaliation.

The war hierarchy should feel believable: warriors exist as a civic necessity, not as the default center of the universe. Peacetime patrols, defense roles, and command relationships are more important than heroic duels.

War should leave permanent marks on the world through scars, ruins, displaced people, and remembered losses.

## Classes

HeelKawn uses classes as social roles, professions, or civic functions rather than RPG power fantasies.

Examples include:

- Farmer
- Builder
- Warrior
- Scholar
- Trader
- Healer
- Artisan

These roles should emerge from settlement needs, labor pressure, skill growth, and cultural expectations. A class is a way society organizes work and consequence, not a shortcut to special status.

Class identity should support:

- Work allocation.
- Training and apprenticeship.
- Social hierarchy.
- Settlement resilience.
- Long-term knowledge transfer.

## Persistent State

All major mechanics should leave a trace in world memory or derived history. That includes births, deaths, migrations, fires, ruins, teachings, wars, and settlement changes.

If a mechanic does not change the record, it probably does not matter enough to belong in HeelKawn.

## Determinism Rule

Same seed plus same inputs should lead to the same mechanics outcome. Any randomness must stay inside seeded, named streams and should never create untracked historical drift.

## Practical Scope

For current project work, the mechanical priorities are:

1. Economy that reflects real storage and labor.
2. Magic that behaves like structured knowledge.
3. War that emerges from world pressure.
4. Classes that reflect social reality rather than power fantasy.

Those four systems should support the wider story of Prometheus instead of competing with it.
