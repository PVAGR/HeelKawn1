AGES — HOW TIME IS STRUCTURED IN HEELKAWN
================================================================================

HeelKawn is divided into Ages.

An Age is not a level, chapter, or season.
It is a period of relative stability where civilization, culture, and memory
can accumulate before conditions make continuation impossible.

An Age ends when systems fail faster than people can repair them.
This may include (but is not limited to):
- Environmental collapse
- Knowledge fragmentation
- Loss of social trust
- Runaway violence
- Resource exhaustion
- Slow institutional decay

The end of an Age is called a Cataclysm.

Cataclysms are not punishments.
They are regeneration events under pressure.

When a new Age begins:
- Most structures are lost
- Some systems are simplified or distorted
- A small number of high-impact remnants persist

What survives an Age is determined by impact, not intent.
This is selective persistence.

Survivals may appear as:
- Altered geography
- Ruins and landmarks
- Biological mutations (plants, animals)
- Cultural customs or taboos
- Incomplete or incorrect myths

No Age fully understands the ones before it.
History degrades into form, not explanation.

Mood event types:
- Joy: +50 mood impact (eating meat, accomplishment)
- Sorrow: -80 mood impact (death of colony members)
- Stress: -40 mood impact (mining injuries)
- Triumph: +100 mood impact (hunting animals)
- Despair: -150 mood impact (crisis situation)
- Contentment: +30 mood impact (normal work completion)
- Boredom: -20 mood impact (repetitive tasks)
- Dread: -60 mood impact (combat/danger sensing)

Crisis level system:
- Calculated from mood + despair events
- Low: < 30% (pawns work normally)
- Moderate: 30-60% (minor issues)
- HIGH: 60-80% (work slowdowns)
- CRITICAL: > 80% (pawns go on strike)
