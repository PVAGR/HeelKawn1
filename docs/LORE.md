# HeelKawn Lore

## Overview

HeelKawn takes place on Prometheus, a world defined by persistence, consequence, and the long shadow of remembered history. The setting is not built around prophecy or chosen heroes. It is built around ordinary people whose actions accumulate into culture, settlement, faction, and legacy.

The core lore principle is simple: recorded fact comes first, and meaning is derived afterward. That keeps the world deterministic while still allowing myths, legends, and interpretations to emerge from actual events.

## Cosmology

Reality in HeelKawn is described through two important pressures:

- Asha: continuity, coherence, restraint, stewardship, and lawful alignment.
- Druj: distortion, fracture, pressure, and entropic drift.

These are not gods. They are world-internal currents that shape how events are interpreted and how societies react to loss, repair, and rebuilding.

The soul-cycle language of the setting moves through Life, Death, the Veil, and Pergatory. That cycle is part of the myth structure of the world, but it never overrides deterministic simulation truth.

## Prometheus

Prometheus is a persistent fantasy planet where settlements are shaped by memory, scarcity, conflict, and recovery. Regions can carry scars, quiet rebuilding, or defensive pressure depending on what history left behind.

The world is meant to feel readable:

- Places remember what happened there.
- Settlements change posture based on repeated events.
- People inherit relationships, reputation, and obligations.
- Ruins and recovery both matter.

Prometheus is not a static map. It is a historical process.

## People And Identity

HeelKawnians begin as ordinary beings. There are no chosen ones and no destiny exemptions. A person becomes significant through labor, teaching, conflict, memory, or enduring cultural impact.

Identity is carried through:

- Bloodline and family memory.
- Personal reputation and social consequence.
- Knowledge preserved or lost across generations.
- Regional history and settlement culture.

That means a pawn is never just a unit. A HeelKawnian can become a builder, a fighter, a teacher, a keeper of knowledge, a founder, or a remembered ancestor depending on what the world records about them.

## Settlements And Regions

Settlements emerge from lived pressure rather than author fiat. Some places become open and adaptive, some become defensive, and some collapse into ruin or later revive.

The current world bible uses region archetypes such as:

- The Quiet Ring: a recovering settlement belt with low scar density.
- Red Scar Basin: a heavily impacted corridor with repeated conflict and abandonment.

These are not cosmetic labels. They represent durable historical patterns that should affect revival, faction identity, and settlement behavior.

## Factions

Factions are emergent identities formed from settlement history, cultural memory, and repeated patterns of survival. They are read-only interpretations of facts, not pre-scripted allegiances.

Examples of canonical faction seeds include:

- Open Hearth Clusters: outward-facing recovery and trade.
- Stoneward Enclaves: defensive, perimeter-minded, loss-aware societies.
- Cautious Steadings: balanced groups that value adaptation and knowledge retention.

Faction identity comes from history, not from a menu.

## Narrative Tone

HeelKawn should feel like a world where small acts can become lasting history. A meal can save a village. A teaching can preserve a generation. A war can reshape a region. A ruin can become a monument.

The tone is grounded, persistent, and culturally layered. It should never drift into generic power fantasy, spectacle-first storytelling, or a morality-meter universe.

## Canon Boundaries

This document is lore reference, not runtime truth. If implementation contradicts the lore, the current state docs and the code path decide what is real. Lore should be used to guide direction, not to overwrite deterministic behavior.
